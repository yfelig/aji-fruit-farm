The Place

This is a 15-acre mango farm in Embilipitiya — deep in Sri Lanka's south, far from the tourist trail. Ajith built four private lakeside cottages himself, placing each one carefully among the mango trees so the orchard stays intact and the farm stays a farm.

Each cottage sits completely on its own, surrounded by trees, with a private veranda facing the water. Raw concrete walls, warm wooden ceiling, sliding glass doors. And a bathroom open to the sky — you shower outside, under the stars.

This isn't a resort. There's no lobby, no pool, no gift shop. What there is: a real lake, a working mango orchard, fireflies after dark, and a family that will make you feel like you've been here before.

Meet Aji

Ajith grew up on this land. The farm, the lake, the mango trees — this is where he's from. He designed the cottages himself, built them with his own hands, and thought carefully about how guests could feel at home in a place that has always been his home.

He'll cook Sri Lankan meals if you ask the day before — rice and curry that guests write about long after they've left. He'll get you on a kayak, point you toward a safari at Udawalawe National Park, or simply leave you alone if that's what you need.

He has two kids, and a warmth that guests consistently call the best thing about the stay.
